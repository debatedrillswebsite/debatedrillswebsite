The Team

Paras Kumar

Debate changed my life. I had the opportunity to compete in Lincoln-Douglas debate from 2007-2011 at Rancho Bernardo High School. Lincoln Douglas debate taught me how to think, write, ask questions, research, and speak in public. Through debate, I learned the value of hard work.

As a competitor, I received 5 bids my senior year and was an octofinalist at the 2011 TOC. Since then, my clients have, amongst many other accomplishments, finished 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 5th, and 9th at the consensus national championship: the Tournament of Champions (TOC). They have also been ranked the 1st, 2nd, 4th and 5th best speakers at the TOC.

I want everyone to be able to benefit from debate the way I have. I was an independent debater without a program, so I know first hand the high barrier to entry in LD. I strongly feel that argumentation and logic should be taught to all students, and this website is my contribution to that cause.

Amit Kukreja

Amit was a four-year member at Science Park High School, competing both locally and nationally. He also was a member for the 2015-2016 National USA Debate team. Amit won the Harrison, Newark, Byram Hills, Bronx, and California round robins. During his senior year, he was a semifinalist at Emory and NDCA, a finalist at the Berkeley Invitational, and won top speaker at Greenhill, Emory, and NDCA. While having a primary focus in LD, he also received a policy bid to the TOC, and won the New Jersey State Championship in policy. He ended his career in Octofinals of the TOC, and will be attending Rutgers Business School in the fall majoring in finance.
